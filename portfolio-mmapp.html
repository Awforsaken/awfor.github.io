Concept design for Manchester Metrolink App

Included Paper sketches, Wireframes, Guerrilla usability testing, High Detail Design, UX and Interactive prototype

Introduction

The idea for this concept design came as a practice exercise for entire lifecycle of concept prototype with exploration for new and better functionality as a current app at the time felt really dated and failed to provide much relevant information. at the same time, it served as a chance to practice guerrilla user testing and how to get best out of it. Most of the improvements were made based on research of current app(at the time) user complaints and frustrations.

Life Cycle

Tested this at multiple stages of the lifecycle, mapped out functional requirements and solutions and then ran it past colleagues and lectors at the University. Then used what i learned to draft initial rough wireframe on paper, which after peer review was ported into low fidelity functional prototype and tested. Based on feedback improved functionality of the prototype and added a bit more detail. After some more guerrilla testing(tested by 3users, in 20min sessions on unsuspecting uni students) and moved onto adding Metrolink branding to prototype and all the cool navigation colours and so on. Then moved on testing it on the real device and real people waiting for trams.

Prototype and branding

Since Manchester Metrolink has very strong brand identity it was fairly easy to make use out of that and build some branded custom interface elements such as arrows and loaders. On top of that Metrolink has very vibrant brand colour which mixed with some branded imagery(used metrolink maps overlayed on top of brand yellow background) provided the app with character. Final conceptual prototype is available at this axShare link.
